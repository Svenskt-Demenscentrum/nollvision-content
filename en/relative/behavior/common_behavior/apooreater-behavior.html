A poor eater

The person with dementia might eat less than before. This might be because they forget to eat. It can also be because they no longer remember how to use a knife and fork, or that they don’t recognize the food. This is not unusual and could be a result of the disease.

What shall I do?

- Make sure there is peace and quiet during meals. Turn off the TV, radio or other sources of sound.

- Reduce impressions and stimuli. Only set the table with what is going to be used during the meal. Too many things on the table can cause confusion.

- Serve food in an appetizing way. Use contrasting colours, for example a coloured plate if serving white fish. This will make it easier to see the food.

- Avoid asking questions or talking too much, this will make it easier to concentrate on eating.

- Offer a snack like a piece of fruit, biscuits, ice-cream or a sandwich several times a day.

- Remember that it’s important with regular visits to the toilet.

- If necessary, contact and visit a dentist. Pain from sores, fungal infections or a damaged tooth may be causing the problem.

- Do the medicines need to be reviewed? Some medication can affect both taste and appetite. Contact the health centre and talk to a doctor about a drug review.

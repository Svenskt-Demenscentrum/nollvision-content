Can I lock the door?

“My wife is very anxious and always wants to be with me. Before, when I have left home she has sometimes gone out and not found her way back. In spite of it not feeling right, I now lock the front door when I go out.”

It is not unusual for people with dementia to have difficulty with orientation. Damages to the brain make it difficult to recognize one’s surroundings. This can be difficult for a caregiver to cope with.

According to the law, it is basically never allowed to lock someone up, whatever the reason is. In special circumstances, if it’s a matter of preventing an urgent hazardous situation, it may be necessary.

What shall I do?

- What is the situation like today? Do they have enough to do, household duties, listening to music or something else?

- Give them a simple cell phone that is easy to use.

- Contact the needs assessor in your council for other possible solutions, like daily walks, home help service or a daily activity.

- Contact the occupational therapist at the health care centre and discuss solutions such as a GPS-alarm or an emergency transmitter. These are aids to help find a missing person. Consent is necessary if these are to be used.
